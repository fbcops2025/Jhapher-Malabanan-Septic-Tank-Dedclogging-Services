Yard warning signs

Unexpected wet ground near the tank deserves a closer look.

A damp patch after rain can be normal surface water. Persistent soggy ground, odor, or lush wet spots near the system should not be ignored.

Warning signs · Practical guide

Do not dig toward the tank or open covers to investigate. Your job is safe observation and clear reporting.

Compare with recent weather

If the area stays wet long after surrounding ground dries, mention that timing. Persistent moisture is more useful than a one-day rain puddle.

Note odor and indoor symptoms

Wet ground plus wastewater smell, slow indoor drains, or backup history strengthens the case for assessment.

Keep the area restricted

Treat uncertain wet spots as off-limits for kids, pets, and foot traffic until the cause is clearer.

Do not excavate on your own

Hidden lids, pipes, and unsafe openings are easy to disturb. Leave uncovering and inspection to professionals.

Send safe visual context

A photo from a distance, landmark notes, and a description of size and smell help more than risky close contact.

Need the area assessed?

Share location, how long the ground has stayed wet, and any indoor drainage symptoms.

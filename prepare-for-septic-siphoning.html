Booking guide · Practical guide

Most delays happen before the work starts: unclear location, covered manholes, tight parking, or missing symptom details. A little preparation reduces back-and-forth.

Confirm the exact property details

Share city, barangay, subdivision or building name, gate instructions, and a useful landmark. For apartments or commercial sites, include the unit or wing if relevant.

Locate the manhole or access point

If you know where the septic access is, take a safe photo. If it is covered by tiles, soil, or storage, mention that early so access can be discussed.

Check vehicle and hose approach

Note narrow driveways, low clearance, parking limits, or long distance from the street to the tank access. Practical access affects planning.

Describe current symptoms

Mention slow drains, odor, overflow history, or whether the visit is preventive maintenance. This helps confirm whether siphoning alone is the likely scope.

Decide who will be present

Make sure someone can provide entry, point out the access, and confirm any on-site questions during the visit window.

Property managers · Practical guide

You do not need a complex facility system. You need dated service notes, clear tenant rules, and a fast way to gather symptoms when something goes wrong.

Keep a one-page service record

Store last siphoning date, access location notes, contractor contacts, and any recurring issues per unit or building.

Set clear flush and grease rules

Tell tenants what not to flush and what not to pour. A simple move-in note prevents repeated avoidable clogs.

Ask for early symptom reports

Encourage tenants to report slow drains and odor early, not only full backups. Early reports are cheaper than emergency mess.

Confirm access before scheduling

Know who has keys, where the manhole is, and whether parking is restricted. This shortens confirmation time.

Decide urgency thresholds

Overflow, wastewater return, or multi-unit slowdown should trigger immediate coordination rather than waiting for a routine slot.

Need help for a rental property?

Send the property location, tenant-reported symptoms, and any last service date you have on file.

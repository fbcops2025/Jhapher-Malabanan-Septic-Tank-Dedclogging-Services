Warning signs · Practical guide

Outdoor odor is easier to dismiss than indoor backup, but it is often an earlier and cheaper time to ask for help.

Pinpoint the strongest smell zone

Yard corner, manhole area, downslope spot, or near a floor-drain exit? Location narrows possibilities.

Check for matching indoor symptoms

If indoor drains are also slow or smelly, include that. Combined symptoms change the likely priority.

Consider recent weather and usage

Heavy rain, guests, or a long gap since maintenance can all be useful context.

Avoid unsafe inspection methods

Do not open tanks or lean into openings to investigate odor. Request professional assessment instead.

Send concise evidence

A short note with location, timing, and safe photos is enough to start a useful conversation.

Persistent outdoor odor?

Request assessment and share whether the smell is near the access point, yard, or both.

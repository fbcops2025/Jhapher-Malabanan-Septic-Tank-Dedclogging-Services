Seasonal care · Practical guide

Rain does not create every septic problem, but it can make existing capacity or drainage issues more noticeable. Track changes instead of assuming it will pass on its own.

Watch for new wet areas

If ground near the tank or drainage path becomes unusually wet or soggy after rain, note it and keep people clear of unsafe spots.

Compare indoor drainage before and during storms

If toilets and floor drains become slower only during heavy rain, include that timing when you request help.

Separate stormwater concerns from wastewater signs

Flooding from poor surface drainage is different from wastewater backup. Describe color, odor, and which fixtures are involved.

Protect access points when possible

Keep manholes identifiable and free from debris so service can proceed if assessment is needed. Do not open them casually.

Request help if odor or backup appears

Persistent odor, indoor backup, or wastewater return should not wait for the weather to improve.

Seeing rainy-season warning signs?

Send photos of wet areas when safe, plus notes on indoor fixture behavior during rain.

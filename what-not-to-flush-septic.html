Prevention · Practical guide

Many backups start with everyday items that seem harmless. Wipes, thick paper, grease, and non-flushable products can accumulate and restrict flow.

Keep non-flushables out of the toilet

Wipes labeled flushable, sanitary products, cotton pads, dental floss, and paper towels do not break down like toilet paper and can create stubborn clogs.

Treat kitchen grease as a drain risk

Cooking oil and grease can coat pipes and trap debris. Let grease cool and dispose of it with solid waste instead of washing it down.

Be careful with food scraps

Rice, coffee grounds, and fibrous scraps can build up in kitchen lines. A strainer helps more than repeated hot-water shortcuts.

Watch household chemical overload

Repeated harsh chemical dumping is not a maintenance plan. It can create hazards and still leave physical blockages in place.

Create a simple house rule

Post a short note in rental units or staff restrooms: toilet paper only, no wipes, no grease. Prevention is cheaper than emergency disruption.

Already dealing with a clog?

If fixtures are already slow or blocked, request assessment rather than waiting for a full backup.

Prevention checklist

  • No wipes or sanitary products in the toilet
  • No grease poured into sinks
  • Use sink strainers where practical
  • Remind household members or tenants

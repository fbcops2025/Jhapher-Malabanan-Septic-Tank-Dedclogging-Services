Septic care · Practical guide

Siphoning restores working capacity by removing accumulated waste. How the household treats drains afterward still matters.

Ask what was completed

Before the team leaves, confirm the agreed scope and any follow-up notes about access, recurring issues, or related drainage concerns.

Resume normal use thoughtfully

Follow any practical guidance given for your property. Avoid immediately challenging the system with grease, wipes, or heavy unnecessary load.

Keep prevention habits active

Toilet paper only, no wipes, no grease dumping, and early reporting of slow drains remain useful after service.

Watch for returning warning signs

If multi-fixture slowdown, odor, or backup returns quickly, report it. A different restriction may still need attention.

Record the service date

Write down when siphoning happened. Future assessment becomes easier when history is clear.

Symptoms returning after service?

Share what changed, which fixtures are affected, and the recent service date so the next step can be assessed.

Aftercare checklist

  • Save the service date
  • Confirm any follow-up notes
  • Keep non-flushables out
